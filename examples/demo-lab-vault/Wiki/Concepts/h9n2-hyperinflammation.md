# H9N2 hyperinflammation

The concept the flu sub-track is built around, synthesized from [[Sources/Articles/Gui_2017_calcitriol-h9n2-inflammation]]
and the figure reads in [[FIGURE_FINDINGS]].

## What it is
H9N2 avian influenza drives a dysregulated cytokine response in the lung. Two temporally distinct waves:
- **Early pro-inflammatory spike (day 2):** TNF-α (~29-fold), IL-2, IL-4, IL-6 — the "cytokine storm" character.
- **Delayed antiviral response (day 4):** IFN-β (~55-fold) peaks later.

## Why it matters
The magnitude and *timing* of these waves track with lung pathology. Calcitriol blunts the early
spike but is associated with worse *later* pathology — suggesting the early response may be partly
protective, and that immunomodulation timing matters.
