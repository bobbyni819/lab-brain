# Gui et al. 2017 — Calcitriol and H9N2 inflammation

Authors: Boxiang Gui, Qin Chen, Chuanxia Hu, Caihui Zhu, Guimei He · *Virology Journal* (2017) 14:10 ·
DOI [10.1186/s12985-017-0683-y](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12985-017-0683-y) · **CC-BY 4.0**.

## Abstract (key points)
H9N2 influenza elicits a hyper-inflammatory response contributing to disease severity. Calcitriol
(1,25-(OH)₂-D₃) modulates immune responses. In A549 cells calcitriol reduced IL-6, IFN-β, and the
viral M gene; in BALB/c mice it downregulated inflammation at day 2 but **increased** it at days 4–6,
and worsened later lung pathology.

## Key results (with source location)
- Fig 5 (qPCR fold-change, days 2–8): TNF-α, IL-2, IL-4, IL-6 peak day 2; IFN-β peaks day 4.
- Table 1: lung-injury scores higher in calcitriol-treated at days 4–6.
- Text states IL-6 "upregulated up to 5.6 fold" and VDR "≈2.5-fold" — most other magnitudes are only in the figures.

## Verified Claims (exact quotes)
> "the levels of IL-6 were upregulated up to 5.6 fold in calcitriol-treated infected mice at 4 days post-infection." (Results)
